Paediatric Dose Guide

Paracetamol & Ibuprofen

⚕️ Always consult your doctor before giving any medication. This guide is for reference only.
When to give Paracetamol: Fever above 38°C, teething pain, minor aches. Give every 4–6 hours, max 4 doses/day. Never exceed recommended dose.

🚨 Emergency Guide

What to Do in a
Child Emergency

Quick first-aid reference for common paediatric emergencies. Click any card for step-by-step guidance. Call 108 for life-threatening emergencies.

Kid's Care Clinic Emergency Line

For non-life-threatening paediatric emergencies

📞 02637-258994 🚑 108 Ambulance
🚨 CALL 108

High Fever (Above 104°F / 40°C)

Especially if child is unresponsive, has seizures, or has neck stiffness

+
  1. 1
    Remove extra clothing. Keep the child in light cotton clothing. Do not bundle up.
  2. 2
    Lukewarm sponge bath. Use a damp cloth on forehead, armpits, and groin. Never use ice cold water.
  3. 3
    Give Paracetamol (15 mg/kg) if child is conscious and can swallow. See Dose Guide above.
  4. 4
    Keep child hydrated. Offer ORS, water, or coconut water frequently in small sips.
  5. 5
    Rush to clinic or call 108 if fever persists above 104°F or child has convulsions.
⚠️ Do NOT give Aspirin to children — it can cause Reye's Syndrome.
🏥 VISIT CLINIC

Febrile Seizure (Fit with Fever)

Sudden jerky movements, stiffness, eyes rolling, temporary unresponsiveness

+
  1. 1
    Stay calm. Most febrile seizures last less than 5 minutes and stop on their own.
  2. 2
    Lay child on a flat, safe surface on their side (recovery position). Remove nearby sharp objects.
  3. 3
    Do NOT restrain the child or put anything in their mouth. Do not give water during the seizure.
  4. 4
    Time the seizure. Note start and end time — tell the doctor this information.
  5. 5
    After the seizure: Child may be sleepy — this is normal. Visit Kid's Care Clinic immediately after.
⚠️ Call 108 if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes or child does not regain consciousness.
🚨 CALL 108

Choking / Breathing Difficulty

Child cannot breathe, cough or speak, turns blue, panicking

+
  1. 1
    For infants under 1 year: Give 5 back blows between shoulder blades, then 5 chest thrusts. Repeat.
  2. 2
    For children over 1 year: Give 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver) from behind the child.
  3. 3
    Call 108 immediately while performing maneuvers. Keep trying until obstruction is cleared or help arrives.
  4. 4
    If child becomes unconscious: Begin CPR and continue until ambulance arrives.
  5. 5
    Visit clinic after clearing to ensure no damage to throat or airway.
⚠️ Never perform blind finger sweeps inside the mouth — it can push the object deeper.
👁️ VISIT EYE DOCTOR

Eye Injury / Foreign Body in Eye

Object in eye, chemical splash, impact injury, sudden vision change

+
  1. 1
    Do NOT rub the eye. This can worsen the injury. Keep child's hands away from eye.
  2. 2
    For foreign body: Flush eye gently with clean water or saline for 10–15 minutes from inner corner outward.
  3. 3
    For chemical splash: Flush immediately with large amounts of water for 20 minutes. Call 108 and visit clinic urgently.
  4. 4
    For impact injury: Apply a cold compress (not ice directly on eye). Do not apply pressure.
  5. 5
    Visit Dr. Vibha Shah immediately. Eye injuries can worsen rapidly without proper treatment.
⚠️ Any eye injury in a child is urgent. Contact Kid's Care Clinic at 02637-258994 immediately.
🚨 CALL 108

Accidental Poisoning / Swallowed Object

Child swallowed medication, household chemical, sharp object, or coin

+
  1. 1
    Stay calm. Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by a doctor — it can cause more harm with corrosives.
  2. 2
    Identify the substance. Save the container or note the name of medication, quantity, and time of ingestion.
  3. 3
    Call 108 or Poison Control. In India: 1800-11-6117 (National Poison Control). Follow their instructions.
  4. 4
    For sharp objects or coins: Rush to nearest hospital. Most pass naturally but X-ray is required to confirm.
  5. 5
    Visit Kid's Care Clinic for follow-up even if child appears fine after the incident.
⚠️ Keep all medicines, cleaning products and small objects out of reach of children at all times.
🏠 HOME CARE

Severe Diarrhea & Dehydration

More than 5–6 loose stools/day, dry mouth, no tears, sunken eyes, extreme weakness

+
  1. 1
    Start ORS immediately. Give WHO-recommended ORS (Electral / Enerzal) in small frequent sips — 50–100 ml after each loose stool.
  2. 2
    Continue breastfeeding if child is on breast milk. Do not stop feeds during diarrhea.
  3. 3
    Give Zinc tablets (as prescribed) for 14 days to reduce severity and duration of diarrhea.
  4. 4
    Watch for danger signs: Blood in stool, high fever, signs of severe dehydration (no urination for 6+ hours, sunken eyes).
  5. 5
    Visit clinic if diarrhea doesn't improve in 24 hours or child shows signs of dehydration.
✅ ORS is life-saving — always keep a packet at home for emergencies.
